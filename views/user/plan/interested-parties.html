Interested Parties

All interested parties should be identified for consideration during the planning of a BCMS.

BCMS Implications:
It is imperative to identify the interested parties so that they can get involved in the process of risk assessment and implementation of the BCMS. Later in clause 4.2.1: Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties; the BCMS team should analyze the security needs of the interested parties and confirm that the organization responds to their concerns. This activity may be conducted via questionnaires, interviews or by facilitating focus groups. Lastly the BCSM project team should define what is expected from all identified interested parties (stakeholders) within the project roles, responsibilities and level of participation required.

The organization must be aware of all those who have an interest in the organization, such as the media, the public, competitors etc... The organization has the obligation to inform all stakeholders of actions and improvements related to Business Continuity that could have an impact on them with such a level a detail that is appropriate to the circumstances. This will be addressed in the section on training, education and communication.
