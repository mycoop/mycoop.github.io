Legal and Regulatory Compliance

In creating and maintaining a BCMS it is vital that a full understanding is gained of the various legal and regulatory requirements that apply to the Organization and its business. This will ensure that the organization continues to meet its obligations in the event of a disruptive incident and its interested parties are not exposed to the risk of criminal prosecution or corporate liability.
The purpose of this section is to document how such requirements are identified and incorporated into the BCMS and how updates to the requirements are managed.
Organizations in multiple locations often need to satisfy requirements in different jurisdictions. ISO 22301 can be used comply to several laws and regulations.
Below are examples of laws and regulations that affect four main industry sectors:
  • Healthcare
  • HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)
    • Requires data backup plan
    • DR Plan
    • Emergency Mode Operation Plan
    • Requirements for Electronic Records
  • FISMA (Federal Information System Act 2002)
    • Emphasis on data security
  • COG (COOP and Community of Government July 1999)
    • Establishes minimum planning considerations for federal government operations
  • NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)
    Special publications (SP) 834, Contingency Planning Guide for Information Technology Systems, July 2002
    • Defines detailed recommendations requiring contingency, DR, and COOP plans
  • FFlEC (Federal Finance Institutions Examinations Council Handbook)
    • Specifies accountability for organization-wide contingency plans and requires timely resumption of operations in the event of a disaster
  • BASEI ll Accord, June 2004
    • Requires banks to implement BC and DR mans to limit losses and ensure continuous operation
  • Interagency Paper on Found Practices to strengthen the resilience of the US Financial System, 2003
    • Emphasis focus on systemic Risk
    • Requires testing of BCPs to incorporate Risk Discovery as a result of the WTC disaster
  • EFAA (Expedited Funds Availability Act), 1939
    • Requires Federally Chartered financial institution to have a comprehensible BCP that ensures prompt availability of funds
  • GASB (Governmental standards Board) No. 34
    • Requires a BCP for agency mission continuity in time
  • NERC (North American Electric Reliability Council 1200)
  • FERC (Federal Energy Regulatory commission)
    • Mandates Recovery Plans
  • RUS 7 CSR Part 1730
    • Emergency Restoration Plan required as condition of continued borrowing
  • Telecommunication Act of 1996
  • NERC Security Guidelines for Electricity Sector
